    - Inferring gene functions by homology and literature search
    - Working in a team and integrating results with other groups

    The building blocks, structures and organelles of the cell are studied and form the basis for discussions about the chemical processes that take place in the cell and contribute to its function. Chemical nomenclature is studied and applied with a focus on the biomedical subject area. The importance of functional groups in biomolecules is discussed and related to what drives chemical reactions in and outside the cell. Acid-base theory is discussed and concepts such as pH and the pKa are related to the biology of the cell. Chemical bonds in and outside the cell are compared and bond strength is related to reactivity and stability. A large part of the course is devoted to the genetic information carriers of the cell and how this information can be transferred in cell division and protein synthesis.
