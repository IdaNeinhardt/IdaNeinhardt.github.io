My Projects

iGEM

During 2024, I competed in the international synthetic biology competition iGEM. Every year, over 400 teams from all over the world gather to take part in the competition where the goal is to come up with a solution to a local problem using synthetic biology.

My team decided to create a novel pesticide that targets an essential developmental gene in the Asian hornet, an invasive species of hornet that hunts local insects and damage fruit crops. To ensure specificity of the pesticide, we created a box in which we aimed to place the bait. The box was outfitted with motion detector sensors and cameras that with an image recognition software only were to open the doors to the bait if an Asian hornet was observed...

Summer Research Scholarship

Between my first and second year of my BSc programme, I applied for, and was rewarded the The Summer Research Scholarship from Lund University. I got the opportunity to undertake a research project in Daniella Rylander Ottosson's lab group focusing on investigating the possibility to reprogram glial cells into interneurons in the brains of mice.

Working under supervision of Srisaiyini Kidnapillai, I got to learn a lot of basic wet-lab protocols and about in vivo experiments.

Summer Research School with Biomedical Orientation

The summer of 2019, I gained my first insights into biomedical research when I participated in the Summer Research School with Biomedical Orientation organised at the Karolinska Institute in Stockholm. I did my project in Dasiel O. Borroto-Escuela's lab investigating the effects of serotonin and dopamine on a specific receptor complex in rat models of schizophrenia.

During the research school, I gained a lot of insights into how research is conducted and how to look for information in scientific literature.
