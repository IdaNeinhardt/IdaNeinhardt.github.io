My Projects

iGEM

During 2024, I competed in the international synthetic biology competition iGEM. Every year, over 400 teams from all over the world gather to take part in the competition where the goal is to come up with a solution to a local problem using synthetic biology.

My team decided to create a novel pesticide that targets an essential developmental gene in the Asian hornet, an invasive species of hornet that hunts local insects and damage fruit crops. To ensure specificity of the pesticide, we created a box in which we aimed to place the bait. The box was outfitted with motion detector sensors and cameras that with an image recognition software only were to open the doors to the bait if an Asian hornet was observed...

First Step Project

As part of my Master's programme, I did a First Step project in the Fasshauer lab under the supervision of Aysima Hacisuleyman. My project revolved around identifying the specified pathway in which SNARE protein complexes tend to disassemble. I utilised molecular dynamics and steered molecular dynamics to simulate the disassembly processes, and built an autoencoder that analyses stiffness matrices, looking for anomalies and building stiffness propagation pathways.

In addition to the scRNA-seq analysis work, I performd RNAscope experiments. I ended up finding fibroblast-like cells in both the glioblastoma dataset used in the bioinformatic analysis and the mouse model used during wet-lab experiments. A subtype of these cells with a myofibroblastic phenotype was also shown to correlate with a worse prognosis for patients with the disease.

During this project, I learned how to plan my own experiments, balancing both wet-lab and dry-lab work.
