iGEM

2024 I competed in the international synthetic biology competition iGEM. Every year, over 400 teams from all over the world gather to take part in the competition where the goal is to come up with a solution to a local problem using synthetic biology. My team decided to create a novel pesticide that targets an essential developmental gene in the Asian hornet, an invasive species of hornet that hunts local insects and damage fruit crops. To ensure specificity of the pesticide, we created a box in which we aimed to place the bait. The box was outfitted with motion detector sensors and cameras that with an image recognition software only were to open the doors to the bait if an Asian hornet was observed...
